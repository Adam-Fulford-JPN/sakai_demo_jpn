Sakai, Osaka Prefecture

Kofun burial mounds

Sakai is famous for its many burial mounds dating back to the Kofun period between the 3rd and 7th centuries. Most impressive of all is an especially massive ancient mausoleum called the Daisen Kofun, which is believed to be the resting place of Emperor Nintoku. In this photo taken from the air, Daisen Kofun is the dark green area at the top right. You can see smaller kofun, too. The Daisen Kofun site is off-limits to the public, but you can view it from the 21st-floor observatory at City Hall. You can also walk or run around the giant kofun's 3-km perimeter. And you can even eat it in the form of a kofun-shaped dessert or monaka wafer, a traditional Japanese confection.

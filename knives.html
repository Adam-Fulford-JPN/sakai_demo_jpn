A history of innovation

Sakai, Osaka Prefecture

Knives

Sakai in the 15th and 16th centuries was a gateway to cultural influences from countries as distant as Portugal. In the 16th century, Portuguese traders brought tobacco, and the metalworkers of Sakai developed excellent knives to cut it. As time passed the knives evolved to be perfectly adapted to cut specific kinds of fish, such as mackerel or skipjack tuna. These days, the kitchen knives made in Sakai are used by some of the world's top chefs.

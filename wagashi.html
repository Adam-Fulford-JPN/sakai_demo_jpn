A history of innovation

Sakai, Osaka Prefecture

Wagashi

In the 15th and 16th centuries, Sakai was a city of sophisticated culture with a focus on the Way of Tea. Many people admired tea-related items with elaborate Chinese motifs. Sen no Rikyu, a native of Sakai, instead emphasized simplicity and seasonal awareness. Wagashi confectionery, whose sweetness offset the bitterness of matcha, could be fashioned into flowers and other items that evoked a sense of nature and the season. For more information, visit the Denshokan Museum, where they sometimes organize wagashi demonstrations. It's a 10-minute walk from Sakai Station.
